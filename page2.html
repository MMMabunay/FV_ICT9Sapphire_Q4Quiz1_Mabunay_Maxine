Noli Me Tangere

Characters in Noli Me Tangere
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin (Ibarra)
  • A wealthy young man of mixed Spanish and Filipino ancestry who has recently returned to the Philippines from Europe after spending seven years studying abroad. Ibarra is cultured and well-respected, though the friars in his hometown of San Diego are suspicious of him.
  • He is the loving son of Don Rafael Ibarra.
  • He fulfilled his father's wish to build a school for Filipinos.
Maria Clara
  • Maria Clara was a lovely, compassionate young lady. Maria Clara was adored by everyone since she had been raised with affection, and because of this, she was able to inspire others to do good deeds, as in the instance of the leper.
  • She is the daughter of Kapitan Tiago and Pia Alba but Tiya Isabel raised her
Padre Damaso
  • Padre Damaso was a brash and arrogant individual. Due to the power afforded to him for being a friar, he acted superior to others and was opposed to natives receiving education abroad.
  • He lacks self-control when speaking. He constantly criticizes or berates those around him, especially Ibarra.
Don Rafael Ibarra
  • He is the father of Crisostomo Ibarra.
  • He was the wealthiest man in San Diego, but he was also the most moral and giving. He therefore stepped on the ruling class, who then plotted his demise. Donya Piá Alba, the mother of Maria Clara and the wife of Kapitán Tiago, passed away while giving birth to her child.
  • Padre Dámaso began to refer to Ibarra's father in his sermon as a result. Shortly after, Don Rafael saw a government tax collector assaulting a little youngster. He accidentally killed the collector when he intervened, which led to his imprisonment.
Kapitan Tiyago
  • He is a wealthy native Filipino who during that period interacted with influential people. He is a representation of the wealthy Filipinos that exploit their own people in order to obtain power and wealth through their affiliations.
  • Maria Clara's father, Kapitan Tiago, was a prominent businessman in San Diego. Kapitan Tiago strained to follow the friars' instructions and betrothed his daughter to Crisostomo Ibarra
Donya Pia Alba
  • Donya Pia Alba is the wife of Kapitan Tiyago and the mother of Maria Clara. She represents our nation, which perpetually yields to foreign force. According to what is known in the community, Kapitan Tiago is Maria Clara's father.
  • She was a wealthy, powerful woman who helped her husband build their business by having him acquire land in San Diego.
Tiya Isabel
  • The cousin of Kapitan Tiago as well as Maria Clara's aunt and primary caregiver. In the De Los Santos homestead, she serves as the chief housekeeper.
  • She helped his cousin Kapitan Tiyago to take good care Maria Clara since she was a child.
Padre Salvi
  • A Spanish friar arrives in the Philippines to work for the Catholic Church. The events of "Noli Me Tangere" take place in the imaginary town of San Diego, where he is assigned. The community recognizes Padre Salvi as a leading figure in no time.
  • The successor of Padre Damaso. Possessing a rivalry with the town's alferez, he later orchestrated the downfall of Crisostomo Ibarra, manipulating the townspeople in the process.
